McDonald's is a multinational fast-food restaurant chain that is one of the largest and most well-known in the world. The company specializes in serving a variety of fast-food items, with a menu that typically includes hamburgers, cheeseburgers, chicken products, french fries, breakfast items, soft drinks, milkshakes, and desserts. Founded in 1940 by Richard and Maurice McDonald as a barbecue restaurant in San Bernardino, California, McDonald's Corporation underwent a transformative shift in 1948 with the introduction of the Speedee Service System. In 1954, Ray Kroc, a Multimixer milkshake machine sales agent, recognized the potential of the McDonald brothers' model and became their franchising agent. The first McDonald's franchise opened in Des Plaines, Illinois, in 1955, marking the beginning of the company's global expansion.
